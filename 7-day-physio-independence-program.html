WHY THIS PROGRAM

Why This Program Was Created

Many patients travel from distant areas to receive physiotherapy at Aarav Clinics. Regular long-distance travel can become difficult because of pain, limited mobility, work schedules, family responsibilities, travel expenses and the challenge of bringing elderly patients repeatedly.

The Aarav 7-Day Physio Independence Program is designed to reduce that burden through supervised physiotherapy, exercise training and practical home-recovery education.

Less Travelling Reduce unnecessary repeated travel.
Correct Exercise Learning Learn proper technique.
Continue at Home Follow your prescribed recovery safely.
HOW THE PROGRAM WORKS

Your 7-Day Physiotherapy Journey

Every patient's treatment plan is personalised. The following illustrates a typical program flow.

1

Detailed Assessment

Evaluation of pain, posture, strength, balance, movement, daily functional activities.

2

Pain & Mobility

Supervised physiotherapy to improve comfort, movement quality, safe mobility.

3

Exercise Training

Personalised exercises introduced according to your condition.

4

Technique Correction

Improve posture, breathing, exercise form, movement quality.

5

Daily Activities

Walking, stairs, transfers, standing, sitting, functional practice.

6

Independent Practice

Patient or caregiver performs the routine while the therapist corrects errors.

7

Home Recovery Plan

Exercises, precautions, frequency, progression, recommended follow-up.

Clinical Note

The seven-day flow is indicative. The physiotherapist may change the sequence, intensity, or number of sessions based on the diagnosis, pain level, medical history and response to treatment.

PATIENT EDUCATION

What You Will Learn

Our goal is not only to provide physiotherapy sessions, but also to help you understand how to continue your prescribed recovery safely at home.

Correct Exercise Technique

Perform your prescribed exercises with proper posture, control and movement.

Repetitions & Sets

Understand how many repetitions, sets and sessions are appropriate.

Activities to Avoid

Know which movements should be avoided temporarily during recovery.

Body Mechanics

Use correct posture while walking, sitting, standing and lifting.

Warning Signs

Recognise excessive pain, swelling, weakness or symptoms requiring review.

Exercise Progression

Progress exercises only when advised by your physiotherapist.

CAREGIVER TRAINING

Helping Families Support Recovery

When a patient requires assistance, one caregiver may be trained in safe exercise support, positioning, walking assistance, transfers, fall prevention and important precautions.

  • Safe walking assistance
  • Transfer techniques
  • Exercise supervision
  • Positioning support
  • Fall prevention measures
  • Home safety guidance

This may be especially useful for elderly patients, children, stroke rehabilitation and people with limited mobility.
