About ABA Therapy

Building Meaningful Skills

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) uses observation and structured teaching to help children develop meaningful communication, daily living, learning and safety skills. Therapy is coordinated with communication, sensory, educational and medical needs.

  • Communication Skills
  • Daily Routine Training
  • Safety Skills
  • Learning Through Positive Teaching
  • Parent Guidance
  • Individualised Goals

Assessment

How Assessment is Planned

01

Understand goals and concerns.

Approach

Person-Centred Practice

  • Positive teaching methods
  • Communication-first approach
  • Respect dignity, assent and breaks
  • Consider sensory and medical needs
  • Regular progress reviews
  • Goals adapted when needed

Data & Progress Review

Progress data supports decision-making but is always considered alongside family feedback, clinical judgement and the child's wellbeing.

Coordination With Other Services

ABA-based support may complement speech therapy, occupational therapy, school services and medical care rather than replace them.
