About Speech Delay

Understanding Speech & Language Development

Speech delay is a broad term describing children who are not developing speech or language skills as expected for their age. Assessment looks beyond the number of words spoken to understand communication, understanding, play and interaction.

  • Individual Communication Assessment
  • Speech & Language Evaluation
  • Play-Based Therapy
  • Parent Coaching
  • Functional Communication Goals
  • Progress Monitoring

Therapy

Treatment & Parent Strategies

  • Functional vocabulary development
  • Sentence building activities
  • Speech sound practice
  • Alternative communication when appropriate
  • Parent language modelling strategies
  • Communication opportunities in daily routines

Why Hearing Matters

Temporary or persistent hearing differences can affect speech and language development. Hearing assessment may be recommended even when a child responds to some sounds.

When Further Evaluation Is Needed

Loss of skills, developmental concerns, feeding difficulties, seizures or significant hearing concerns should be reviewed by appropriate medical or developmental professionals.
