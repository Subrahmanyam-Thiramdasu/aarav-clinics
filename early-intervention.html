About Early Intervention

Supporting Development From an Early Stage

Early intervention helps children with developmental concerns in communication, movement, play, learning, behaviour and daily routines. Therapy focuses on identifying needs early, supporting families and building practical everyday skills.

  • Communication Development
  • Motor & Movement Skills
  • Sensory Regulation
  • Play & Learning
  • Family-Centred Goals
  • Coordinated Care
When to Seek Support

Developmental Concerns We Assess

Speech Delay

Limited gestures, words or communication.

Motor Delays

Balance, coordination and milestone concerns.

Play & Attention

Participation and interaction difficulties.

Sensory Regulation

Challenges during daily routines.

Autism Concerns

Developmental assessment and support.

Loss of Skills

Prompt developmental or medical review.

Family-Centred Goals

Goals focus on meaningful participation in communication, play, dressing, eating, movement and classroom routines while reflecting family priorities.

Review & Coordination

Services are reviewed regularly and adjusted according to progress, ensuring coordinated care rather than unnecessary therapy sessions.
